Quinn

About

My name is Quinn, I am a low-level software engineer. At the very least, I inspire to be. I just love learning about a multitude of subjects, and not the surface level stuff either!

I am aware that of the knowledge that I have on software development, there is so much that I haven't learnt yet. However, I do recognise that I tend to go deeper into the material than my peers.

The professional / personal overlap

I strongly agree and stand behind the philosophies of open-source software. I love using GNU/Linux as my operating system of choice. Where within engineering, I focus the most around game development. As I find that the most engaging as I get to be creative as well, and produce a piece of art. That doesn't mean that I don't or won't work in other fields. I can say I am quite interested in system engendering, so take from that what you will. One of the private projects is my home linux server, which I haven't had the chance to talk about much. My current knowledge and what I am planning to learn

I am currently quite knowledgable in C and C#, python have used often in the past, but the reasons to use it started diminishing a ton as bash scripting does most of what I used it for nowadays (light scripting). I quite dislike working with C#. My experience working with it described in a sentence is: "A bloated, high boilerplate, little system control and object oriented paradigms first language.". OOP is not bad per-say. I believe it gets to shine in the places where it actually is useful, usually it isn't, though.

My favourite language at this point by far is C. I am still learning, but I love it's flexibility, minimalism and just how close you work with the hardware.

I am planning to tackle x86_64 assembly, as it is an invaluable tool whilst debugging and invaluable to know what is happening further under the hood. I am also planning to learn C++ and then Rust. I chose that order because when I got started on Rust, I struggled a lot with the unfamiliar syntax on top of the new concepts. I reconised that C++ has some parallels, so getting comfortable in that first is likely a good call.
